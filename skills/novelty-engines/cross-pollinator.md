# Cross-Pollinator — Solutions from Distant Fields

Every field has solved a version of your problem. You just don't know what they call it.

## Protocol

**Step 1: Abstract the Problem.** Strip domain-specific language. Describe it in the most general terms.

**Step 2: Import from 15 Fields.** Astrodynamics, Epidemiology, Immunology, Monetary Policy, Marine Biology, Game Theory, Thermodynamics, Evolutionary Biology, Neuroscience, Civil Engineering, Jurisprudence, Music Theory, Military Strategy, Agriculture, Anthropology.

**Step 3: Extract the Mechanism.** Not the surface analogy — the underlying dynamic.

**Step 4: Map Back.** Construct a concrete application to the original problem.

**Step 5: Rank.** Novelty × Feasibility × Impact.

## Example

**Problem:** "How do I reduce hallucination in LLMs?"
**Abstracted:** "Prevent a system from generating outputs that don't correspond to reality."
**From Immunology:** Train a secondary "immune system" model that learns which hallucinations are dangerous for the specific deployment context.
**From Civil Engineering:** Build a "safety factor" — multiple independent generation paths for critical claims.
**From Jurisprudence:** Burden of proof — LLM must cite sources for factual claims; perform "discovery" by searching for contradictory evidence.
