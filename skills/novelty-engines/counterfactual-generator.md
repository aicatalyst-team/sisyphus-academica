# Counterfactual Generator

The most-cited paper is not necessarily the best — it's the one that got the most attention.

## Protocol

**Step 1: Identify Canonical Papers.** The 3-5 most-cited in the subfield.

**Step 2: Remove Each One.** Imagine it was never published.

**Step 3: Trace Ripple Effects.** What immediately changes? What problems remain unsolved? What other approaches would have been explored? What would have been invented instead?

**Step 4: Identify Suppressed Alternative.** The approach actively suppressed by the canonical paper's success.

## Example

**Field:** Deep Learning
**Canonical paper:** AlexNet (2012)
**Suppressed alternative:** Capsule Networks. Hinton's alternative to CNNs was never seriously explored because CNN dominance absorbed all attention and funding.
**Plausibility:** Moderate. Capsule nets are expensive, but a decade of optimization might have made them practical.
