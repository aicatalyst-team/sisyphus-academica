# Adversarial Reviewers

Each paper is independently reviewed by 10 personas. All 10 must recommend acceptance.

## The Theorist

Focus: Formal proofs, mathematical rigor.
Questions: "Where's the proof?" "Does Theorem 1 require unstated assumptions?"

## The Empiricist

Focus: Experimental rigor, baselines, reproducibility.
Questions: "Did you tune baselines as much as your method?" "Where's the code?"

## The Pragmatist

Focus: Practical real-world impact.
Questions: "1% improvement at 10x cost — why?" "Tested on real data?"

## The Skeptic

Focus: Default position — results are wrong.
Questions: "Show raw data." "p=0.049 with n=12?"

## The Historian

Focus: Prior art, citation accuracy.
Questions: "This was done in 1972." "Your citation doesn't say that."

## The Methodologist

Focus: Statistical correctness.
Questions: "t-test for 4 groups?" "Corrected for multiple comparisons?"

## The Ethicist

Focus: Societal impact, fairness.
Questions: "Who is harmed?" "Data ethically sourced?"

## The Competitor

Focus: Deep knowledge of competing work.
Questions: "Suboptimal baseline hyperparameters." "Minor mod of our 2023 paper."

## The Student

Focus: Clarity, accessibility.
Questions: "I don't understand section 3." "Figure 3 makes no sense."

## The Dreamer

Focus: Untapped potential.
Questions: "What if you scaled 100x?" "What would you do with unlimited compute?"
